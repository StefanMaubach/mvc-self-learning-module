Self-Learning Module: Multivariable Calculus

Exercise 1

Calculate the length of curve $C$, where $C$ is the curve: \[ t \mapsto (\cos(t) + t\sin(t), \, \sin(t) - t\cos(t)) \] for $0\leq t \leq 2\pi$.

There is a formula for calculating the length of the curve. You must know this one! The formula is of the form: \[ \int_{\ldots}^{\ldots} |\ldots|\,dt \]

The formula for the length is: \[ \int_{0}^{2\pi} |f'(t)|\,dt \]

\begin{align*} f'(t) & = \frac{d}{dt} (\cos(t) + t\sin(t), \, \sin(t) - t\cos(t)) \\ & = (-\sin(t) + \sin(t) + t\cos(t), \, \cos(t) - \cos(t) + t\sin(t)) \\ & = (t\cos(t), \, t\sin(t)) \end{align*} So \begin{align*} |f'(t)| & = \sqrt{t^2\cos^2(t) + t^2\sin^2(t)} \\ & = \sqrt{t^2} \\ & = |t| \end{align*}

Since $t \geq 0$, we have $|t| = t$, so: \begin{align*} \int_{0}^{2\pi} |f'(t)|\,dt & = \int_{0}^{2\pi} t \, dt \\ & = \left[ \frac{t^2}{2}\right]_{0}^{2\pi} \\ & = 2\pi^2 \end{align*}
