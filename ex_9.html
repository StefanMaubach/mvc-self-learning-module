Self-Learning Module: Multivariable Calculus

Exercise 9

A rocket is flying through space. At time $t$, for $t \geq 0$, it is at location \[ f(t) = \left(t\cos(t),\: t\sin(t),\: \frac{2\sqrt{2}}{3}\,t\sqrt{t}\right). \] At which point $(x,y,z)$ is the rocket's speed equal to 37?

Compute a formula for the speed of the rocket at time $t$.

The velocity of the rocket at time $t$ is given by: \[ f'(t) = \left(\cos(t)- t\sin(t),\: \sin(t) + t\cos(t),\: \sqrt{2t} \right) \] The speed is given by $|f'(t)|$. Calculate (and simplify) this!

\begin{align*} |f'(t)| & = \sqrt{\left(\cos(t)- t\sin(t)\right)^2 + \left(\sin(t) + t\cos(t)\right)^2 + \big(\sqrt{2t}\big)^2} \\ & = \sqrt{\cos(t)^2 - 2\cos(t)\,t\sin(t) + t^2\sin(t)^2 + \sin(t)^2 + 2\sin(t)\,t\cos(t) + t^2\cos(t)^2 + 2t} \\ & = \sqrt{t^2\left(\sin(t)^2 + \cos(t)^2\right) + 2t + \left(\sin(t)^2 + \cos(t)^2\right)} \\ & = \sqrt{t^2 + 2t + 1} \\ & = \sqrt{(t+1)^2} \\ & = |t+1| \\ & = t+1, \end{align*} since $t\geq 0$.

\[ 1 + t = 37 \implies t = 36 \] At $t=36$ the rocket is at point: \begin{align*} f(36) & = \left(36\cos(36),\: 36\sin(36),\: \frac{2\sqrt{2}}{3}\cdot 36\sqrt{36}\right) \\ & = \left(36\cos(36),\: 36\sin(36),\: 144\sqrt{2}\right) \end{align*}
