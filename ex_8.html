Self-Learning Module: Multivariable Calculus

Exercise 8

Consider the following 5 points: \[ A = (0, 0, 0), \quad B = (2, 0, 0), \quad C = (4, 4, 0), \quad D = (4, 0, 4), \quad E = (0, 0, 4) \] Find all the points that are equidistant from $A,B,C,D,E$.

Let $P=(x,y,z)$ be such a point. Write down equations for the distances of $P$ from $A,B,C$ to be equal, i.e.: \[ \left|\overrightarrow{PA}\right|^2 = \left|\overrightarrow{PB}\right|^2 = \left|\overrightarrow{PC}\right|^2 = \left|\overrightarrow{PD}\right|^2 = \left|\overrightarrow{PE}\right|^2 \]

\[ \left|\overrightarrow{PB}\right|^2 = (x-2)^2 + (y-0)^2 +(z-0)^2, \] etc.

\begin{align*} (x-0)^2 + (y-0)^2 + (z-0)^2\ = & \ (x-2)^2 + (y-0)^2 + (z-0)^2 \\ = & \ (x-4)^2 + (y-4)^2 + (z-0)^2 \\ = & \ (x-4)^2 + (y-0)^2 + (z-4)^2 \\ = & \ (x-0)^2 + (y-0)^2 + (z-4)^2 \\ \implies & \\ x^2 + y^2 + z^2\ = & \ x^2 + y^2 + z^2 - 4x + 4 \\ = & \ x^2 + y^2 + z^2 - 8x - 8y + 32 \\ = & \ x^2 + y^2 + z^2 - 8x - 8z + 32 \\ = & \ x^2 + y^2 + z^2 - 8z + 16 \\ \implies & \\ 0\ = & \ - 4x + 4 \\ = & \ - 8x - 8y + 32 \\ = & \ - 8x - 8z + 32 \\ = & \ - 8z + 16 \\ \end{align*}

We have: \[ 0 = -4x + 4 \implies x=1 \] For $x=1$: \[ 0 = - 8x - 8y + 32 = -8y + 24 \implies y = 3 \] For $x=1, y=3$: \[ 0 = - 8x - 8z + 32 \implies z = 3 \] But now for $x=1$, $y=3$, $z=3$: \[ -8z + 16 = 0 \implies -8 = 0, \] contradiction! So there are no points equidistant from $A, B, C, D, E$.
